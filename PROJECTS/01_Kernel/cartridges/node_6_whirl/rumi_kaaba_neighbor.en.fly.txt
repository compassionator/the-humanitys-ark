O you who’ve gone on pilgrimage, where are you, where?
Here, here is the Beloved! Come now, come here!

Your beloved is your neighbor, just next door,
Why wander in the desert? What do you explore?

If the formless face of the Beloved you see,
You are the Kaaba, the house, and the key.

Why seek what was never lost, why wander away?
In search of what's not gone, where do you stray?

A hundred times from this roof to that house you have gone,
Once from that house to this roof, be drawn.

If your true aim is to see the Kaaba of the soul,
Polish the mirror of your heart, where the Beloved's light makes you whole.

Where is a single flower if that garden you've seen?
Where is the heart's pearl if in God's sea you've been?

With all this, your pain shall become your gain,
Alas, you are a veil on your gain, causing the pain!
