The Sufi laid his snare, his tricks to expose,
Plotting deceit with the cosmos, as it goes.
The wheel of fate shall break his egg beneath his hat,
For he dared his tricks on the wise who see through that.
Come, O heart, let us seek the Divine’s embrace,
From those with short sleeves, yet greedy hands in disgrace.
Do not deceive, for love without truth is in vain,
It locks the heart’s door, where meaning should reign.
When truth’s face reveals itself on judgment’s day,
Ashamed shall be the seeker, who walked the false way.
O partridge, strutting with grace, where do you stray?
Do not be fooled; even the pious cat once knelt to pray.
Hafez, blame not the free souls who wander away,
For God freed us from pious pretensions on creation’s first day.
