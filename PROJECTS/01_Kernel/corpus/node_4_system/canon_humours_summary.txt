Avicenna describes four humours as by-products of digestion/aliment with distinct qualities:

Blood: forms from aliment of equable temperament.

Bilious humour: an attenuated by-product described as hot (also “sweet/oily/sharp”).

Serous humour: a dense, humid/viscid, cold by-product.

Atrabilious humour: a very dense by-product, deficient in moisture, associated with excess heat (and also linked to cold in a “congelative” excess context).

He also states the formation rule in terms of heat/cold balance: “When the heat is equable, blood forms …” and when heat goes into excess, bilious humour forms; when cold is equable, serous humour forms; when cold is excessive toward congelation, atrabilious humour forms.
