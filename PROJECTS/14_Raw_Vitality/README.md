# Raw Vitality

## Role
Seed / Research

## Goal
Preserve an archival research record and personal hypothesis centered on breath, food, movement, and self-observation, with special attention to the 40-day raw-eating lineage associated with Arshavir Avanesian and later lived practice.

## Public-use boundary

Raw Vitality is archival research and personal hypothesis. It is not public medical advice, treatment guidance, or an operational protocol. It must not be used to diagnose, treat, or cure disease, and it must not be used to change medication or professional care. The historical protocol is preserved for provenance and future qualified review, not as instructions for public self-experimentation.

## Why it matters
This project exists to preserve a serious hypothesis:

- damaged breathing, damaged diet, and reduced movement may be major drivers of decline
- oily, fried, processed, animal-heavy, and chemically burdened intake may weaken long-term vitality and possibly immune resilience
- a recurring 40-day raw plant-based cycle may improve vitality, clarity, and recovery for some people
- this may also have downsides, limits, or failure cases that need honest tracking

The goal is not to erase uncertainty.
The goal is to preserve the protocol clearly enough to test it honestly.

## Current next step
Organize source provenance, historical claims, uncertainty, and safety questions for future qualified review. Do not recruit or direct public experiments.

## Status
Archival research / personal hypothesis

## Source lineage
Current source materials preserve a strong tradition that ties many forms of decline to three recurring factors:
- breathing
- diet
- movement

They also preserve a recurring intervention pattern:
- a 40-day raw plant-based cycle
- removal of animal products
- removal of cooked/processed foods
- removal of many “chemical” inputs
- closer return to natural conditions and observation

This lineage is associated in the source material with Arshavir Avanesian, later lived witnesses, and the preservation of related health insights carried forward through figures such as Seyyed Khalil.

## Preservation direction
This project is not only about self-testing.
It is also about preserving, translating, and comparing the raw-eating lineage:
- Arshavir

- later personal witnesses
- future scientific cross-checks

## Research boundary
This project preserves strong claims because they matter.

But it will hold them in the right form:
- hypothesis
- protocol
- witness
- tracking
- comparison
- future validation target

Not:
- blind dogma
- guaranteed cure promise
- forced conclusion before testing

## What this project is trying to test
1. Do processed, oily, fried, and highly artificial diets reduce vitality over time?
2. Do they increase inflammatory burden, sluggishness, or immune weakness?
3. Does one 40-day raw cycle improve energy, digestion, clarity, or recovery?
4. What downsides appear?
5. Who responds well, poorly, or not at all?
6. Which parts of the protocol matter most:
   - rawness
   - plant-only
   - reduced processing
   - breathing
   - movement
   - adherence
